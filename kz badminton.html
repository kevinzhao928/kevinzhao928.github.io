My relationship with badminton:

I started playing badminton in 2007, when I was 8 years old. It was introduced to me by my parents as I was extremely overweight when I was younger. I found badminton to be quite fun as it took my mind off golfing. I never trained professionally or had a badminton coach; I just played against people that were better than me. By the time I got into highschool I was pretty decent at it. It was in Gym class during badminton where I met my best friend to this day: Raphael Lee. We joined the badminton team and quickly formed a close friend group consisting of Raphael, Patrick, Jack, and I. Outside of school we would always go to Richmond Pro to practice and play against each other and the 4 of us became the core of our badminton team. For the next 3 years, we constantly played against each other and against the best players in Vancouver. During my time on the team - from grade 8→10 we were crowned city champions twice.

After I left Prince of Wales to attend the International Baccalaureate program, I continued to play and was able to obtain the City Champions title for my new highschool. The group of players I met at Churchill are nowhere near as close as my friends from Prince of Wales however they are still my teammates and I have immense respect and appreciation for everyone on the team.

At university, I do not plan on joining the varsity badminton team however I still play casually on drop-in nights and as apart of the University of Waterloo Badminton Club.
